# How It Works

**Four steps. No app. No new hardware for pupils to carry.**

---

## 1. We tag what needs tagging

A small NFC tag attaches to the item — a phone pouch, a kit bag, a piece of equipment. It's discreet, durable, and doesn't change how the item's normally used.

## 2. The tag is registered to your school

Not the pupil. Not a parent. Your school is the point of contact from the moment the tag goes live — so there's no new consent process and no new data collected from families.

## 3. Someone finds it and taps their phone on the tag

Any modern smartphone reads an NFC tag with a simple tap — no app to download, no account to create. This works whether the finder is a pupil, a parent, or a stranger on a bus.

## 4. Your school gets notified, and forwards it on

You receive the notification first. From there, you contact the parent using the details you already hold — the same way you'd handle any other lost-property call.

---

## What this replaces

Most schools currently rely on one of these:

- A lost-property box that gets checked occasionally, if at all
- Pupils writing their name on the item in marker pen
- Nothing — the item just doesn't come back

None of these tell you an item's been found the moment it happens. iFoundIt does.

---

## What it doesn't do

- It doesn't track location in real time. This is a "found and notified" system, not a live tracker.
- It doesn't require the finder to have anything installed. If their phone can tap to pay, it can read the tag.
- It doesn't put any pupil data in a finder's hands. The finder only ever reaches your school.
