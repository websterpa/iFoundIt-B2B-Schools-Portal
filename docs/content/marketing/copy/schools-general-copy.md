# For Schools

**Tag what your pupils carry. Get more of it back.**

Schools lose more than phone pouches. Kit, coats, bags, water bottles, equipment — all of it ends up in a lost-property box that most families never check. iFoundIt tags close that gap, without adding a new app or a new data burden for your school to manage.

---

## Where iFoundIt fits

### Phone pouch protection
If your school uses locking pouches, a lost pouch outside school still finds its way back. Tags register to your school, not the pupil — so recovery doesn't depend on the finder knowing who owns it.
**[See pouch protection →]**

### PE kit and equipment
Kit gets left in changing rooms, on buses, at fixtures. Tagged kit means a find gets reported to you directly, instead of sitting in lost property until someone happens to check.

### Coats, bags and everyday items
The bulk of what fills a lost-property box every term. Tagging the items pupils carry daily cuts down on what never gets claimed.

---

## Why the school is the contact, not the pupil

Every use case above works the same way:

- The tag is registered to your school.
- A finder taps their phone on the tag — no app needed — and your school is notified.
- You forward the notification to the parent, using contact details you already hold.

This means no new consent process, no new data collected from families, and no pupil-facing account to manage or lose access to.

---

## What this saves you

- **Less replacement spend.** Every item found instead of replaced is money back in your budget — kit, pouches, and equipment all carry a real replacement cost.
- **Less admin.** Notifications go straight to your school, rather than routing through a lost-property box nobody checks.
- **No new procurement decision.** Tags add to equipment and pouch orders you're already placing.
